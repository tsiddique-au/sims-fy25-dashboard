FY26 Market Outlook — Sims Management View
Management Outlook Signals — FY26
🏭
EAF Growth & Scrap Demand — Long-Term Structural Tailwind
Electric arc furnace capacity globally continues to expand, underpinned by decarbonisation mandates and government onshoring policies. Scrap demand growth is a multi-decade structural trend that directly benefits Sims' core business across NAM and ANZ.
Positive
🤖
AI Data Centre Cycle — SLS Demand Surging
Hyperscaler data centre expansion is accelerating hardware refresh cycles, driving sustained demand for SLS IT asset disposition services. Resale prices for repurposed units are lifting. SLS is well positioned to build on its record FY25 EBIT performance throughout FY26.
Positive
🇺🇸
US Tariffs — Domestic Scrap Premium to Persist into FY26
Tariffs protecting domestic US steel and aluminium industries are supporting local ferrous scrap demand and maintaining a domestic sales premium. Management expects this benefit to extend from FY25 into FY26, continuing to support NAM and SAR margins.
Positive
🇨🇳
China Steel Exports — Persistent Headwind, Floor May Be Near
Record-high Chinese steel exports are expected to continue suppressing ferrous prices in export markets, particularly challenging ANZ and NAM export sales. Management notes that while pricing pressure persists, further significant declines from FY25 levels appear limited as markets find a floor.
Watch
♻️
Non-Ferrous Momentum — Strong Markets Expected to Continue
Zorba, aluminium, and copper demand are expected to remain robust throughout FY26, providing strong trading margin contributions across NAM, ANZ, and SAR operations. The structural shift from ferrous to non-ferrous revenue mix (FY25: +7ppts to 33.6%) is expected to continue.
Positive
💧
Decarbonisation Plan Reset — Scope 1 Hard-to-Abate Focus
FY26 will see a comprehensive review of the decarbonisation roadmap. Scope 1 diesel and gas emissions now represent nearly all remaining operational emissions — and are the hardest to abate. Capital will be redirected toward the most impactful initiatives including electrification of plant and vehicles.
In Progress
Executive Summary — FY25 Turnaround & FY26 Watch Points
Sims delivered a decisive earnings recovery in FY25: EBIT +198%, FCF positive at $106.8M, EBITDA +48.1%. The turnaround was real but concentrated — driven by NAM cost discipline and SAR JV contribution, while ANZ structurally underperformed. In FY26, the most critical management tasks are: (1) refinancing the $513M facility before October 2026; (2) resolving the UK Metal receivable ($77M+ exposed); and (3) sustaining SLS momentum while managing China-driven ANZ headwinds. The balance sheet has materially strengthened but gearing remains above target and statutory earnings remain negative. Sims is a business in recovery — the turnaround is credible, but not yet complete.
